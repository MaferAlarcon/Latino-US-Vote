What Does the Hispanic Vote Reflect About the U.S. Presidential Election?

Latino men showed their support for Trump’s campaign, while Latinas supported Harris. Was the gender agenda the turning point for the Latino vote?

November 12, 2024

By: María Fernanda Alarcón

The Latino vote was a surprise in these elections, in which Trump emerged victorious. During the pre-electoral process, both the media and analysts focused primarily on viewing the Latino vote as a homogeneous bloc. However, post-election polls reveal that it was, in fact, diverse and polarized. The political analyst Estefanía Veloz highlights that “Since 2016, there’s been a noticeable shift of Latino voters, particularly men, toward Trump. In this election, Latino men leaned toward him for the first time, while Harris continued to hold a commanding lead among Black men and women.”

This not only sheds light on the effectiveness of the candidates’ campaigns but also reveals the diverse needs of the Hispanic population. Given Trump’s very conservative stance on gender issues compared to Harris, these results also highlight the extent to which the gender struggle exists within Hispanic communities in the United States.
